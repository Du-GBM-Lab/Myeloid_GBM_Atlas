A High-Resolution scRNA Atlas Reveals the Diversity and Baseline Interactions of GBM Myeloid Cells

Integrated Single-Cell and Spatial Multi-Omics Decipher the Myeloid Landscape and Reveal the Myelin-Induced SPP1-CD44 Axis as a Therapeutic Target in Glioblastoma
This study constructs a high-resolution spatiotemporal atlas of the myeloid compartment in GBM, uncovering a pathogenic feed-forward loop wherein tissue damage (demyelination) reprograms macrophages into a Phagocytic Suppressive state via the SPP1-CD44 axis. Our findings provide a unified framework for understanding myeloid heterogeneity and establish the SPP1-CD44 interaction as a pivotal therapeutic target to remodel the immunosuppressive niche and enhance the efficacy of immunotherapy.
